Orange

Oranges, which can range in flavor from sweet to sour, are usually peeled and eaten fresh or pressed for juice. The thick, bitter peel is usually discarded. The outer layer of the peel can be rubbed thinly to make orange zest. The white part of the peel, including the core, is a source of pectin and contains almost the same amount of vitamin C as the pulp and other nutrients.

Grapes

Grape berries usually resemble an elongated ellipsoid of rotation in their appearance. Thus, we have grape fruits or grapes are spherical or ovoid berries that belong to the type of fruit growing in more or less loose clusters, containing between 15 and 300 pieces each and can be of different colors: purple, crimson, black, dark blue, yellow, green, orange and pink.

Gauva

Guava fruits, are round or oval depending on the species. They have a pronounced and typical fragrance, similar to lemon rind but less sharp. The outer skin may be rough, often with a bitter taste, or soft and sweet. The pulp inside may be sweet or sour and off-white to deep pink. The seeds in the central pulp vary in number and hardness, depending on species.
